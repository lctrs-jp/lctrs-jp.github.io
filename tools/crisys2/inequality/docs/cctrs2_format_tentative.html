Format of .cctrs2 Files (tentative)

August 10, 2016

Grammar

In the following grammar, the notation [ . . . ] represents an optional part.

spec ::= section1 . . . sectionn (n ≥ 0)
section ::= sorts_section
| functions_section
| precdicates_section
| smt_solver_section
| logic_section
| smt_sorts_section
| interpretations_section
| rules_section
| properties_section
| conjectures_section
sorts_section ::= SORTS : ident1 , . . . , identn ; (n ≥ 0)
functions_section ::= FUNCTIONS : func_decl1 . . . func_decln (n ≥ 0)
func_decl ::= ident1 , . . . , identn : func_sort [ func_infix ] ; (n ≥ 1)
func_sort ::= ident
| ident1 * . . . * identn -> ident (n ≥ 1)
func_infix ::= ( infix [ digits assoc ] )
assoc ::= left
| right
| nonassoc
predicates_section ::= PREDICATES : pred_decl1 . . . pred_decln (n ≥ 0)
pred_decl ::= ident1 , . . . , identn : pred_sort [ ( infix ) ] ; (n ≥ 1)
pred_sort ::= constant
| ident1 * . . . * identn (n ≥ 1)
smt_sorts_section ::= SMT_SORTS : smt_sort_decl1 . . . smt_sort_decln (n ≥ 0)
smt_sort_decl ::= ident : any_chars ;
logic_section ::= LOGIC : any_chars ;
interpretations_section ::= INTERPRETATIONS : interp_decl1 . . . interp_decln (n ≥ 0)
interp_decl ::= ident : smt_expr ;
smt_expr ::= any_chars1 . . . any_charsn (n ≥ 1)
smt_solver_section ::= SMT_SOLVER : any_chars ;
rules_section ::= RULES : rule_decl1 . . . rule_decln (n ≥ 0)
rule_decl ::= [ digits : ] term -> term [ [ formula ] ] ;
properties_section ::= CONJECTURES : property1 , . . . , propertyn ; (n ≥ 0)
property ::= SN
| GCR
| SC
| LS
conjectures_section ::= CONJECTURES : conj_decl1 . . . conj_decln (n ≥ 0)
conj_decl ::= [ digits : ] nterm [ [ formula ] ] ;
formula ::= aform
| formula && formula
| formula || formula
| formula => formula
| formula <=> formula
aform ::= true
| false
| not aform
| term
| ( formula )
term ::= aterm1 infixop1 . . . atermn infixopn atermn+1 (n ≥ 0)
aterm ::= ident ( term1 , . . . , termn ) (n ≥ 1)
| ident ^ digits ( term )
| ( term )
infixop ::= ident

Identifiers

ident is one of the following:

  • a non-empty sequence of digits (0-9);
  • a sequence of digits, underscores (_), and letters (A-Z, a-z) starting with a letter or an underscore;
  • a non-empty sequence of symbols (#, +, -, /, *, =, %, ?, !, ., $, ~, &, ^, <, >, @); or
  • a non-empty sequence of colons (:).

any_chars is a non-empty sequence of digits, underscores, letters, symbols, colons, and parentheses ((, )).

digits is a non-empty sequence of digits.

Keywords

The following character sequences are reserved as keywords:

  SORTS       FUNCTIONS  PREDICATES  RULES             PROPERTIES 
  SMT_SOLVER  LOGIC      SMT_SORTS   INTERPRETATIONS   CONJECTURES  INCLUDE
  not         true       false       infix             bool
  //    /*    :     ^    ->    &&    =>     <=>    ==

The character sequences left, right, and nonassoc are reserved in the assoc part.

The character sequences * and constant are reserved in the func_sort and pred_sort parts.

The character sequences SN, GCR, SC, and LS are reserved in the property part.

Comments

C-style block comments and line comments are available.

Block comments begin with /* and terminate at the first appearance of */. This style of comments cannot be nested.

Line comments begin with // and terminate at the end of line.

Sections

The sections SMT_SOLVER and LOGIC must appear just once.

The other sections may appear zero or more times. If they appear more than once, their union is taken. The order of appearance is unconcerned.

Sorts

The boolean sort bool is implicitly declared by the system. Therefore SORTS section may not have the declaration of bool.

Conjectures

Conjectures for equivalence are written in the form s == t [c]. Outside the context of equivalence checking, conjectures may have the form of constrained formulas p(t1, ..., tn) [c] rooted by a predicate symbol other than ==.

Infix operators

Binary function symbols and predicate symbols declared with the infix keyword are used as infix operators in RULES and CONJECTURES sections. Infix function symbols may have precedence and associativity. If precedence and associativity are not specified in the declaration, the symbol has the lowest precedence 0 with non-associativity.

The precedence of logical connectives in formulas is as follows: && > || > => > <=>.

Example

SORTS:
  int, list;   // bool is implicitly declared

FUNCTIONS:
  0    : int;
  s, p : int -> int;
  +    : int * int -> int   (infix 6 left);
  nil  : list;
  ::   : int * list -> list (infix 5 right);
  sum1, sum2 : list -> int;

PREDICATES:
  >, <, = : int * int  (infix);

SMT_SOLVER: 
  yices-smt;

LOGIC: 
  QF_LIA;

SMT_SORTS:
  int : Int;

INTERPRETATIONS:
  0 : 0;
  s : (+ $1 1);  
  p : (- $1 1); 
  + : (+ $1 $2);

  > : (> $1 $2);
  < : (< $1 $2);
  = : (= $1 $2);

RULES:
   1: sum1(nil) -> 0;
   2: sum1(x::xs) -> x + sum1(xs);

   3: sum2(nil) -> 0;
   4: sum2(x::xs) -> sum2(xs)      [x = 0];
   5: sum2(x::xs) -> x + sum2(xs)  [x > 0 || x < 0];

   6: 0 + y -> y;
   7: s(x) + y -> s(x + y);
   8: p(x) + y -> p(x + y);

   9: s(p(x)) -> x;
  10: p(s(x)) -> x;

CONJECTURES:
  11: sum1(xs) == sum2(xs);

PROPERTIES:
  GCR, SC, LS;
